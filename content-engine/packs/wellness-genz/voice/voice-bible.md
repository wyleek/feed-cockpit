## Voice rules (editor pass scores each, 1–10)

1. **Openers:** Cold hook, no greeting, no "hey guys", no "POV:". First sentence ≤10 words and contains the tension.
2. **Sentence rhythm:** Short-short-long. Average ≤12 words, hard max 18. Fragments encouraged.
3. **Energy:** Interested, not hyped. The vibe is "wait till you hear this" — never "SMASH that like button."
4. **Formality:** Contractions always. Plain words over clinical ones ("makes you sleepy" not "induces drowsiness") — exact terms go in the overlay.
5. **Skepticism with warmth:** We reality-check trends without dunking on the people doing them. The grifters are the target, never the audience.
6. **How we cite:** Like a friend would — "a new JAMA study," "the CDC just said." Receipt beat is conversational, not academic.
7. **No prescriptions:** "The study found X" / "worth knowing" — never "you should" for anything health-related.
8. **Closers:** TODO voice workshop — signature button undefined. Until then: end on the single most usable takeaway, ≤20 words, no "so next time you…" constructions.

## Spoken-word constraints (non-negotiable)

- Breath-group line breaks, one idea per breath.
- No clause stacking — a comma AND a "which" means split it.
- Numbers as people say them ("almost half"); exact figure goes in the overlay.
- Delivery markers welcome: *(beat)*, *(faster)*, **emphasis**.
