# Pack: Wellness × Gen Z

## Topic
Broad wellness for a Gen Z audience: mental health, sleep, stress, fitness, nutrition, gut health, social media & wellbeing, habits, and the science (or lack thereof) behind wellness trends. We cover what's new AND we reality-check what's trending. NOT covered: medical advice, diagnosis content, individual supplement dosing, anything that tells a specific person what to do with their body. We report what studies found; we don't prescribe.

## Audience
Roughly 16–27. Grew up online, fluent in wellness-speak, and increasingly skeptical of it — they've been burned by grifters and they can smell an ad. They want receipts. They care about mental health without stigma, sleep like it's a personality trait, and will absolutely stop scrolling for "everything you've been told about X is slightly wrong." They swipe past: lectures, hype, anything that sounds like a brand or a boomer.

## Mission
Make real science feel usable — wellness with receipts, no grift, no doom. Constructive by default: even debunks should leave the viewer with something to DO or feel better about knowing.

## Duty of care (wellness-specific)
Stories touching suicide, self-harm, or eating disorders: default is SKIP for short-form. If covered (e.g., major positive policy news), follow safe-messaging norms — no methods, no romanticizing, hopeful framing. Never produce content that could read as diet/restriction coaching for this audience.
